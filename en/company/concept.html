Origins of our name, “Kanamic Network”

Kanamic Network traces its origins to our ideal of developing a network service to breathe life into elderly care, and it is composed of three keywords: “KAigo”　（Japanese for “elderly care”) + "dyNAMIC" + "ICT NETWORK".

Making Japan a Forerunner in Solving the Problems of Super-aged Society

The baby boomers, the generation that shepherded Japan’s growth and development, turned 65 in 2015 and will turn 75 in 2025. This makes Japan the global forerunner in confronting issues related to a super-aged society.

Kanamic Network began as a provider of a system for assisting elderly care practitioners in the year 2000 with the arrival of the elderly care Insurance Law, and has contributed to the implementation of regional comprehensive care through the development of an information sharing system. Kanamic’s model has been adopted by numerous projects including an undertaking by the University of Tokyo’s Institute of Gerontology (IOG) and been subject to numerous trials and proof-of-concept experiments, helping it grow into the model that sets the national standard.

There’s a tendency to have a bleak outlook for the future of a Japan facing various problems stemming from being a “super-aged” society of the likes that’s never been seen on the face of this planet. But Kanamic’s contribution to society is to help build a “longevity” society that we can be proud of.

We work to improve our services in the hope that Kanamic Network can help lay the foundations for a future that not only the elderly but also the generations that support them can smile about.
